2. Course Description

This course is the fundamental for preparing students to understand and apply more vocabulary when encountering subjects related to technology and computing in their respective major. All of the topics are embedded with grammar, vocabulary and skills focus, therefore, students are highly recommended to continue to remember the rules of all grammar topics they have learned. The grammar topics students learn this semester will require them to practice speaking and writing using more complex English sentence structures and to produce technical descriptions and opinion essays. In this second semester, students should be able to comprehend all strategies needed to read longer texts in English to generate good comprehension and critical thinking.

3. Course Outcomes

demonstrate the performance internalizing values, norms and ethics of academics that is independent, in quality and measurable by applying logic, critical, innovative, quality, and measureable thinking for carrying out specific tasks related to their field that should meet standard of competence of their major through collaboration, social sense awareness and caring for people and environment as well as respect for cultural diversity, views, religions, faiths, opinions or others’ original findings either independently or collaboratively by following academic values, norms and ethics

6. Course Content

Unit 1: Gerund and to-Infinitive

Vocabulary study and reading comprehension : Software for food combining

Listening: Students listen to their friends’ reading aloud

Speaking: Mingle discussion on software for food combining

Unit 2: Present Future

Vocabulary study and reading comprehension : Future of Signal apps (https://beebom.com/whatsapp-vs-telegram-vs-signal/)

Listening: Students listen to their friends’ reading aloud

Speaking: Mingle discussion on the most promising apps for future generations

Unit 3. Modals and Phrasal Modals

Vocabulary study and reading comprehension : English learning applications

Listening: Students listen to their friends’ reading aloud

Speaking: Mingle discussion on the best applications for helping speaking and writing practice

Unit 4. Word class

Vocabulary study and reading comprehension: Metaverse

Listening: Students listen to their friends’ reading aloud

Speaking: One minute presentation (random), topic: Metaverse

Unit 5. Certain expressions and the transition markers for describing a pie chart

Writing: Interpreting a pie chart

Unit 6. Prepositions and Phrasal Verbs

Vocabulary study and reading comprehension: Impacts of Gamification in Education

Listening: Students listen to their friends’ reading aloud

Speaking: Mingle discussions on Impacts of Gamification in Education

Unit 7. Transition markers for expressing opinions in an essay

Writing: An Opinion essay (Assignment I)

Unit 8. Relative Pronouns

Vocabulary study and reading comprehension : Analogue computer for the future

Listening: Students listen to audio/video

Writing: Practice sentences using Relative Pronouns

Unit 9. Relative Adverbs

Vocabulary study and reading comprehension: Low/No Code Software Development

Listening: Students listen to audio/video

Speaking: Practice sentences using Relative Adverbs

Unit 10. Noun clauses

Vocabulary study and critical reading: Humanoid

Listening: Students listen to their friends’ reading aloud

Writing: Practice sentences using Noun clauses

Unit 11. Adjective Clause and Reduced Adjective Clause

Vocabulary study and critical reading: Web 3.0

Listening: Students listen to audio/video

Writing: Practice sentences using Adjective clause and Reduced Adjective clause

Unit 12. Adverb Clause and Reduced Adverb Clauses

Vocabulary study and critical reading: Industry 5.0

Listening: Students listen to audio/video

Writing: Practice sentences using Adverb clause and Reduced Adverb clause

Week 14-15: Presentation (Assignment II)
